I love Markdown. There are many reasons for this, but the most important
one is the perfect compromise between $\LaTeX{}$ and plain text. Though
I use both when I have to, the former has a steep learning curve and not
practical for daily life, the latter has too little structure.

I wanted to have a personal website as an extension of my notes. A place
to share what I think about with as little friction as possible. I take
my digital notes locally using [Zettlr](https://www.zettlr.com). I am
using a static site generator, [Hugo](https://gohugo.io). Before that I
was using [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com), but I wanted to have a less
outdated option with zero dependencies. For now my deployment pipeline
is GitHub Pages and Actions. My theme is based on
[bearblog](https://github.com/janraasch/hugo-bearblog/) with little
tweaks here and there.

I post here without leaving my working environment at all, only
exporting my Markdown notes. So far, I am happy!
